# Antipathy is a contrary-planet map, not a humoral recomputation

Treatment by antipathy strengthens the **contrary planet** of the seat's ruling planet, where "contrary" is fixed by **opposing rulership** (opposite domiciles): Sun ↔ Saturn, Moon ↔ Saturn, Mars ↔ Venus, Mercury ↔ Jupiter. This is Culpeper's practiced rule ("diseases of Mars by the herbs of Venus, and the contrary; diseases of the luminaries by the herbs of Saturn…"). Antipathy keys off the afflicting *planet*, not off a humoral quality of the complaint.

The first slices instead recomputed antipathy humorally: take the complaint's hot/cold·wet/dry quality, flip both axes, and return the planets of that exact temperament. We rejected that for two reasons. First, it **contradicts documented practice** for the luminaries — it would pair the Sun (hot/dry) with cold/wet planets, whereas Culpeper pairs the Sun with Saturn (cold/dry). Second, and decisively, it is **not citable**: ADR-0002 makes a verified verbatim quote mandatory behind every derivation, and there is no classical passage that says "a hot-and-dry complaint is met by the cold-and-wet planets" — that sentence was our reconstruction. The contrary-planet pairs, by contrast, are quotable directly from Culpeper. When the trust pillar (citability) and a theoretical reconstruction conflict, citability wins.

The trade-off we accepted: the user is **no longer asked for the humoral nature of the complaint**. Under the humoral rule the complaint's quality was a real input (the picker's second level); under the contrary-planet rule it is inert — antipathy is fully determined by the seat's ruler. So the quality picker is removed and an **Issue collapses to a seat alone** (compounding the earlier removal of `seat.quality`). Humoral qualities survive only as *explanatory commentary* in the rationale (and as citable planet temperaments from Ptolemy), not as the computation. A future reader should not reintroduce a humoral-quality input expecting it to drive antipathy — it deliberately does not.

Consequences for implementation: the picker becomes a single kind of choice (navigate to a seat; no nature step), `COMPLAINT_NATURES` is removed, `Issue` drops its `quality` field, and `humors.ts`'s `contraryOf`/`planetsWithQuality` are replaced by a `contraryPlanetsOf(planet)` map grounded in opposing rulership.
